Introduction

This research uses the Hobday et al. (2016) and Hobday et al. (2018) definitions for marine heatwaves (MHWs) and their categories and was conducted using the {heatwaveR} implementation of the algorithm (Schlegel and Smit 2018). It is not yet known what the primary drivers are of MHWs in the Northwest Atlantic. We use here a methodology from Schlegel et al. (2017) and Oliver et al. (2018) to implement a self-organising map (SOM) to determine the primary synoptic patterns in several key abiotic variables. The data used in this research were from the NAP (3-Oceans) model.

Objectives

  1. Find the primary drivers of MHWs in the Northwest Atlantic
  2. Determine if these drivers occur during certain seasons
  3. Determine if these drivers have relationships with specific regions

Methods

  • Regions created along coast of Atlantic Canada based on Richaud et al. (2016)
  • Sub-regions created based on bottom depth within region
  • SST time series made for each region from average of pixels therein
  • MHWs calculated from each average SST time series
  • Average anomaly abiotic synoptic states created during each MHW
  • Anomalies fed to SOM to produce 12 most common states
  • The occurrence of these states with sub-regions and/or seasons quantified
